# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Combat athletes — amateurs to full-time professionals — aged roughly 18–35. They live in gyms. They are skilled and serious about the sport but financially under-leveraged. They're used to being sold cheap coaching programs and hustle-culture Instagram advice. They are skeptical of marketing, respect authenticity, and respond to directness. They arrive at this page because someone told them about it, or they're already frustrated that less-talented peers are making more money than them.

## Product Purpose

First Light Society turns combat athletes into earning enterprises. It does this through a two-tier service (Spark / Blaze) built on the Fame-to-Fortune Formula™: Brand Amplification, Visibility Acceleration, Monetization Engine. The page's single job is to get an athlete to submit an application — not to buy, but to be considered.

## Brand Personality

Authoritative. Cinematic. Earned.

Not a hype machine. Not a hustle-culture coach. Not a tech-startup. The tone is that of a management agency that already has a track record — calm, direct, slightly exclusive. The brand should feel like the athlete is applying to First Light Society, not First Light Society pitching to them.

## Visual Identity

- **Logotype**: "FIRST LIGHT" in Cormorant SC uppercase, thin Bone rule, "SOCIETY" in Cormorant SC below
- **Monogram**: FL in Cormorant SC on Warm Night square
- **Typography**: Cormorant SC (titles, tier names, display) + Cormorant italic (pullquotes, tier promises) + DM Sans 300/400/500 (body, labels, buttons)
- **Palette**:
  - Warm Night: #12100e (background)
  - Bone: #f0ebe3 (text, form background)
  - Dawn Accent: #3d5a6c (structural, guarantee section, focus states)
  - Amber Accent: #d4925a (CTAs, highlights, founding numbers)

## Anti-references

- UFC / Bellator visual identity (red/black, grunge, aggression)
- Instagram coaching programs (cheap gradients, bold yellow CTAs, testimonial carousels)
- SaaS landing pages (feature grid, metric heroes, light mode with blue CTAs)
- Editorial-magazine aesthetic (Klim-style display serif + drop caps + broadsheet grid)
- Fight-cliché: octagon imagery, gloves, "warrior" language in visual design

## Design Principles

1. **Prove it by being it.** If we're selling brand-building, the page itself is the proof of concept. Every design decision should communicate that First Light Society knows what it's doing.
2. **Authority through restraint.** Premium doesn't shout. The feeling of exclusivity comes from what's left out, not from what's piled on.
3. **The athlete applies, not the other way around.** The CTA framing and page arc should make the athlete feel like they're being considered, not sold to.
4. **Cinematic over editorial.** Drama through scale, atmosphere, and contrast — not through grids, rules, and typographic hierarchy for its own sake.
5. **Content earns the format.** Section structure follows meaning, not template. Numbers when there's a sequence. Labels when they orient. Nothing decorative.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. All form inputs labeled. Reduced motion respected. Sufficient contrast on all text.
